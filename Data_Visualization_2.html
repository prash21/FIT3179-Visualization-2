The Global Pandemic

The Covid-19 virus is an infectious disease that can be easily transmitted through droplets from a person who is infected. The Covid-19 pandemic has affected millions of people worldwide, where until today, some countries are still battling against the waves of infectious clusters. It is therefore vital that we learn how the virus has made an impact globally, and what we can do to stay safe!


Covid-19 Global Deaths and Cases

The Covid-19 virus has spread globally, causing death to millions of people. Here's the death rate and cases for each country around the globe, scaled to per 10,000 people in each country.





Total Deaths and Cases between Continents

Here's how the total number of deaths and total number of cases differ between different countries, as well as different continents. We can see from the chart that the United States has both the most deaths and most cases!





Daily cases in the Top 5 Most Infected Countries

The top 5 countries that were most infected by the Covid-19 virus were France, The United States, India, Russia, and Brazil.

With the chart on the right, you can see the trend of new cases since the start of this year. From the trend, India peaked at nearly 100,000 cases recorded in a single day!

Daily total deaths in the Top 5 Most Infected Countries

Just like the one above, the chart on the left is for the top 5 most infected countries, but for number of deaths instead.

It's worth to point out that while the number of cases have rised in recent months, the death rates have not - possibly due to enhanced virus treatments and the country's preparedness for infection waves.



Median Age Versus Covid-19 Deaths Between Different Countries and Continents

It's no doubt that the Covid-19 virus can spread to people of any age, but does age play a role in the severeness of the virus? Here, we can see that on average, the higher the Median Age of a country, the more number of deaths there are.




Help slow the spread of Covid-19!

With the spread of such an infectious disease, it's best if you learn more and find ways on how to stay safe.

From the chart on the right, we can see that there's nearly 1.5 billion media content items online. This means that there is plenty of information you can find about the ongoing pandemic, in contrast to past pandemics.

Do your part in wearing a mask too!

One of the most effective ways to slow the spread of Covid-19 is to wear a mask in public. Here are some materials that you could consider using as a mask.

In the chart to the left, we can see that using a surgical mask is the most ideal option. It has the highest efficiency, along with a low drop in pressure which means that it's easy to breathe with the mask on too!
